Project 01 · Bachelor's Thesis

Almost Real

AR Furniture Space Configuration App

Augmented Reality Unity 3D C# ARCore Microsoft Azure Addressables Android Bachelor's Thesis · 2022

Overview

What is Almost Real?

Almost Real is an Android AR application developed as my Bachelor's thesis at the Technical University of Sofia. It allows users to visualise 3D furniture models placed in their real environment using their smartphone camera, without ever visiting a store.

Think of it as a personal IKEA Place, but built from scratch. Users scan their room, select furniture from a scrollable catalogue, spawn it into their space, and then move, scale, and rotate it until it feels right, all in real time.

The Problem

Online shopping lacks spatial context

Traditional e-commerce only shows flat images of furniture. Customers can't know if a sofa will fit their living room or match their existing decor until it arrives, often leading to costly returns and frustration.

The Solution

Try before you buy, at home

Almost Real uses markerless AR to scan real surfaces and overlay photorealistic 3D furniture models. Users get a true sense of size, proportion, and style - from their couch, at any time.

Key Features

What users can do
  • Spawn furniture - tap any item from the horizontal scroll catalogue to place it exactly where the AR marker sits.
  • Move it - drag the selected item across the detected plane to reposition it anywhere in the room.
  • Scale it - use the + / − scale buttons to resize the object bigger or smaller.
  • Rotate it - clockwise or anti-clockwise rotation with dedicated controls.
  • Delete it - tap any spawned item and hit "Delete Item" to remove it.
  • Refresh - reset the entire scene and start fresh.
  • Info panel - a built-in guide helps first-time users get started instantly.

Tech Stack & Architecture

How it was built

The app uses Google ARCore via Unity's AR Foundation for markerless plane detection. 3D models were sourced from Sketchfab, imported and configured as prefabs in Unity, then grouped as Addressables - uploaded to Microsoft Azure Blob Storage so they load dynamically at runtime without bloating the APK.

The architecture centres on five core classes: PlacementIndicator (raycast + marker), DataHandler (model list + button creation), InputManagerScript (all touch interactions), AssetLoader (async addressable loading), and Item (scriptable objects holding prefab + sprite data).

Unity 2020.3 AR Foundation ARCore XR Plugin Microsoft Azure Addressables DOTween C# / Scriptable Objects Android 7.0+

Existing Apps

What was missing
  • IKEA Place only includes ~50% of IKEA's actual catalogue and doesn't redirect to the store to purchase.
  • Houzz is powerful but hard to navigate, and its floor plan tool failed in furnished rooms.
  • Neither app gave users a truly seamless try-on-to-checkout flow.

My Approach

What I did differently
  • Built a minimal UI that surfaces the catalogue without obscuring the AR view.
  • Used cloud-hosted Addressables so models load on demand, keeping the app lightweight.
  • Designed full interaction controls (move, scale, rotate, delete) accessible with one hand.

Testing & Outcomes

What I learned from testing
  • Initial object spawn sizes were off -> resolved by calibrating each prefab's scale in the Unity inspector before building.
  • Scene switching broke the AR marker - fixed by attaching the PlacementIndicator script directly to the AR Camera.
  • Azure connection required exact catalog files in the blob container - proper addressables rebuild resolved load errors.
  • Final validation: 3D objects spawn, move, scale, rotate, and delete successfully across multiple Android devices.

Future Scope

Where Almost Real could go next
  • iOS support (currently Android only).
  • Direct integration with a furniture store's live product catalogue.
  • In-app purchase / cart redirect functionality.
  • Multi-language support.
  • Auto-generated 3D models from product images using future ML pipelines.
  • Would definitely redesign it, to a better modern-looking design

Overview

What is Mini-World?

Mini-World is an educational VR game developed as part of the Extended Reality course, built in a team of three. Players are shrunk down to insect size and dropped into a lush virtual garden, a world that feels alive, curious, and full of things to discover.

The game combines exploration, photography, and quiz-based interactions to form a complete learning loop: discover → capture → learn → test. Its main goal is to let children explore the hidden world of insects, helping them discover and understand organisms that are normally beyond our sight. To ensure a safe and immersive experience, the project was specifically designed in VR.

Level 1 - The Garden

Explore & Discover

Players roam a virtual garden and encounter different insects hidden throughout the environment. Interacting with each insect reveals a fun educational fact about it. Players can also take in-game photos of each insect they find, which get stored in their personal journal, building a collection as they explore.

Level 2 - The Quiz

Test Your Knowledge

After exploring the garden, players enter Level 2 - a short quiz that tests what they learned from their insect encounters. The questions are based on the facts discovered during exploration, reinforcing the learning through active recall in a playful, low-pressure format.

Key Features

What makes Mini-World tick
  • Starting menu with level selection - players choose their entry point from a clear menu.
  • Insect interactions - approaching and engaging with each insect triggers a pop-up fact.
  • In-game camera & journal - players photograph each insect, photos are then stored and viewable in a journal.
  • Quiz system - Level 2 presents questions drawn from the facts players encountered in Level 1.
  • Data logging - all player activity is logged to a CSV file for analysis and research purposes.

Project 03 · UI Design

Online Payment Landing Page

Web UI · Banking & FinTech

UI DesignWeb DesignFigmaLanding PageFinTech · 2025

Overview

What is this project?

A fully designed landing page for an online payment and digital banking product. The goal was to build trust instantly, because in FinTech, first impressions directly affect conversion. The design communicates security, speed, and simplicity above all else.

The Challenge

Trust is hard to earn on a single page

Financial products face a uniquely high bar: users need to feel safe before they even consider signing up. The design had to balance boldness with credibility, confident but not aggressive.

My Approach

Clarity over decoration

I led with a strong hero section that names the value proposition upfront, used a restrained colour palette with high contrast CTAs, and let generous whitespace do the trust-building heavy lifting.

Design Decisions

Key choices that shaped the page
  • Typography hierarchy - bold display type for the headline, lighter weight for supporting copy. No visual competition.
  • Colour palette - deep navy with electric accent, signalling reliability and modernity.
  • Social proof positioning - trust indicators (logos, stats) placed close to the CTA to reduce friction at the moment of decision.
  • Card UI for features - scannable benefit blocks that users can read in under 10 seconds.

Project 04 · UX/UI Design

Le Petite Café

Web Design · Café Ordering

UX/UI DesignWeb DesignFigmaCafé · Food & BevPersonal Project · 2025

Overview

What is Le Petite Café?

Le Petite Café is a website designed for a boutique café where customers can browse the menu and place orders directly online. The goal was to create an ordering experience that feels as warm and inviting as walking into the café itself(a virtual café for now), making it easy to find what you want, customise your order, and check out without friction.

The Challenge

Ordering that feels effortless

Most café websites are either too basic or too cluttered. The challenge was to design a clean, intuitive ordering flow that guides users from browsing to checkout in as few steps as possible, without losing the personality of the brand.

My Approach

Warm design, clear flow

I used earthy tones, generous food photography, and a simple layout hierarchy to make the ordering process feel natural. Every screen was designed with one goal: get the user to what they want fast, with no confusion along the way.

Key Screens

What the website covers
  • Home - hero section with featured items, today's specials, and a clear call to order.
  • Menu - categorised by drinks, food, and extras with filters and food photography.
  • Item detail - customisation options (size, milk type, extras), quantity, and add to cart.
  • Cart & checkout - order summary, pickup or delivery selection, and payment.
  • Order confirmation - clean confirmation screen with estimated ready time.

Project 05 · UI Design

Muzic App

Mobile App · Music Streaming

UI DesignMobile AppFigmaNotionMusic · EntertainmentPersonal Project · 2025

Overview

What is Muzic?

Muzic is a dark-mode music streaming app concept that puts the artist's album at the centre of the experience. Designed for people who care deeply about how music looks as much as how it sounds, the UI adapts its colour palette dynamically to the currently playing track, creating an immersive, ever-changing environment.

The Challenge

Standing out in a crowded space

Spotify, Apple Music, Tidal - music apps are dominated by giants. The design challenge was to carve out a visual identity that felt fresh, premium, and genuinely music-first rather than feature-first.

My Approach

Visual immersion as a differentiator

I designed around the music rather than around the interface. Album art bleeds into backgrounds, text adapts to contrast, and the player screen becomes a full-canvas experience, almost like a digital album sleeve.

Key Screens

Core flows
  • Home / for you - personalised recommendations, new releases, recently played.
  • Now playing - full artist's album with adaptive background, waveform scrubber, lyrics toggle.
  • Library - saved albums, playlists, and artists with clean list/grid toggle.
  • Artist profile - discography, top tracks, upcoming events.
  • Search & discovery - genre mood boards and trending charts.

Project 06 · UX/UI Design

Foodz Delivery App

Mobile App · Food Delivery

UX/UI DesignMobile AppFigmaNotionFood DeliveryPersonal Project · 2024

Overview

What is Foodz?

Foodz is a comprehensive food delivery app concept designed from the ground up, covering every stage of the user's journey from restaurant discovery to real-time order tracking and post-delivery review. The design prioritises speed and appetite appeal: food should look irresistible on screen.

The Challenge

Navigating complexity without friction

Food delivery apps carry enormous feature complexity like: menus, filters, cart, payment, tracking, history. The challenge was to keep every flow feeling light and fast, even when handling many options and states.

My Approach

Progressive disclosure

I structured the app so users are never overwhelmed, showing only what's relevant at each moment. The home screen is curated, not cluttered; complexity is revealed gradually as users drill deeper into their order.

Key Flows

What the app covers end-to-end
  • Onboarding - location permission, dietary preference setup, personalised home.
  • Discovery - restaurants by category, cuisine filter, distance & rating sort.
  • Restaurant & menu - scrollable menu sections, item photography, customisation modal.
  • Cart & checkout - order summary, coupon apply, address selection, payment method.
  • Live tracking - map with driver location, estimated arrival, contact driver.
  • Order history & reviews - past orders with re-order shortcut, star + text review.

Project 07 · Web Design · Fashion

Elie Saab - About Page

Luxury Fashion · Editorial Web Design

Web DesignFigmaPinterestFashion · Haute CouturePersonal Project · 2025

Overview

What is this project?

A reimagined "About" page for Elie Saab, one of the most celebrated names in haute couture. The project explores how a luxury fashion house can tell its story online: through restraint, grandeur, and the kind of editorial stillness that lets craftsmanship speak for itself.

Rather than showcasing products, this page is purely narrative, the founder's story, the house's heritage, and the philosophy behind the stitches.

The Challenge

Luxury that doesn't shout

Luxury brands walk a fine line online, the web is inherently noisy, but the brand demands quiet authority. Every pixel choice had to feel deliberate and refined, as if chosen by the atelier itself.

Design Philosophy

Editorial minimalism

Inspired by high-fashion print editorials: full-bleed photography, serif-driven typography with wide tracking, off-white backgrounds, and sparse layout that treats empty space as a design element.

Design Decisions

What shapes the page
  • Typography - classical serif display paired with ultra-light body text. Wide letter-spacing throughout to convey exclusivity.
  • Photography treatment - full-bleed, unclipped images that fill the viewport, demanding the viewer's full attention.
  • Colour palette - ivory, champagne, and charcoal.
  • Layout rhythm - alternating full-width and contained sections create a breathing pace: fast, then slow, then fast again.
  • Storytelling structure - the founder's origin → the craft → the legacy → the present. A narrative arc, not a list of facts.
